Protection

When your PC is compromised by malware, it is imperative to clean it immediately.

Luckily there many tools out there that do just that.

First things first is to always be aware of your PCs behavior
    Does it take longer for your PC to boot up?
    Is it slower in general?
    Does your browser take you to weird sites?
    Are your fans spinning louder than usual?
1. Safe Mode

All of these unusual behaviors can mean your PC has some kind of malware. First thing you should do is restart and enter "Safe Mode" by pressing F8 as soon as it starts to boot up. When your PC is in Safe Mode only the most basic Microsoft programs are running.

2. Delete Temp files

Some viruses hide in the temporary files. To clean them do the following

    Click the Windows logo on the right bottom
    Type “Temporary Files”
    Choose “Free up disk space by deleting unnecessary files”
    Find and select “Temporary Internet Files” in the ‘Files to delete’ Disk Cleanup list and click OK
    Confirm “Delete Files” selection
3. Run a virus scan

Now run a virus scan with an antivirus program. We recommend MalwareBytes and BitDefender.

4. Delete or Quarantine the virus

If a virus is found, it may affect multiple files. Select ‘Delete’ or ‘Quarantine’ to remove the file(s) and get rid of the virus. Rescan your computer to check if there’s no further threats. If threats are found, quarantine or delete the files.

5. Restart the PC

Now that the virus is removed, you can reboot your computer. Simply turn it on as you would normally. It no longer needs to be in ‘Safe Mode’.

If after all this your computer still acts weird, your best option would be to completely restart the system. This means deleting all files, so make a backup of of the most important ones.
