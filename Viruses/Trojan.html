Trojan

Definition

A Trojan horse, or Trojan, is a type of malicious code or software that looks legitimate but can take control of your computer. A Trojan is designed to damage, disrupt, steal, or in general inflict some other harmful action on your data or network.

Types of Trojans

There are various types of trojans. Underneath you'll find a list of the most common types.

  • Backdoor
  • Exploit
  • Banker Trojan
  • Clampi Trojan
  • Cryxos Trojan

Prevention

There are different ways to protect yourself. Actually trojan and phishing attacks have some things in common when trying to protect yourself. But the probably most effective way is being cautious about downloads. You should never download or install software from a source that should not be trusted. Furthermore you should be aware of phishing threats. Don't ever open attachments, click links or run a program sent to you in an email from someone you don't know. A really good tip is not visiting unsafe websites. Most popular websites have security certificates - their URL typically start with https:// rather than http://, the s stands for secure. You'll also find a padlock icon in the adress bar. Also protect your passwords with complex and unique passwords. A strong password is not easy to guess and ideally made up of a combination of upper and lower-case letters and special numbers.
